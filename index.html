About Me

I am a certified Cybersecurity and IT engineering professional with a solid foundation in Python programming, ethical hacking, and modern cybersecurity practices. My expertise is backed by certifications from CompTIA (A+, Network+, Server+, Security+) and Cisco (CCNA), and strengthened by hands-on training from the HyperionDev Cybersecurity Bootcamp.

During my cybersecurity bootcamp, I gained hands-on experience with a range of tools and techniques used in ethical hacking and penetration testing. I learned how to perform reconnaissance using tools like Spiderfoot, theHarvester, Sublist3r, and Assetfinder for email harvesting, subdomain enumeration, and asset discovery. I used Nmap for network scanning and Nessus for vulnerability assessment. Web application testing was enhanced through Burp Suite, where I explored attacks like XXE (XML External Entity) injection. For privilege escalation and post-exploitation on Linux and Windows systems, I worked with LinPEAS, LinEnum, Linux Exploit Suggester, and Windows Exploit Suggester. Finally, I utilized the Metasploit Framework to execute exploits and gain deeper insight into real-world penetration testing workflows.
